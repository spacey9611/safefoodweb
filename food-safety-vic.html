Food Safety Training in Victoria (VIC)

Victoria's food safety is overseen by the Department of Health Victoria under the Food Act 1984 (Vic), with local councils registering and inspecting premises. Food businesses are classified by risk (Class 1–4); Class 1 and 2 premises must appoint a Food Safety Supervisor.

Victoria is unique in offering DoFoodSafely — a free online learning program that helps food handlers build knowledge. It is not a nationally recognised certificate, so anyone needing an FSS qualification still completes SITXFSA005 and SITXFSA006 through a registered training organisation. Who needs food safety training in Victoria?

Victoria classifies food businesses by risk from Class 1 (highest) to Class 4 (lowest). Class 1 and Class 2 premises — most cafés, restaurants, pubs and caterers — must appoint a Food Safety Supervisor who holds the nationally recognised units and is reasonably contactable by staff, and Class 2 businesses also need a documented food safety program. Every other food handler must have skills and knowledge appropriate to their work, which is exactly what this practice test builds.

Registering a food business in Victoria

Fixed premises register with their local council, while temporary, mobile and online vendors register once through the statewide Streatrader portal and then notify each council where they trade. Councils inspect premises and can issue improvement notices under the Food Act 1984 (Vic). Victoria's free DoFoodSafely program is a handy primer, but it is not a Statement of Attainment — for a Food Safety Supervisor role you still complete SITXFSA005 and SITXFSA006 through an RTO.

Preparing for your Victorian assessment

What you are assessed on in Victoria is the national Food Standards Code plus the SITXFSA005/006 unit requirements, so the 650 questions here map directly to your assessment. Focus first on temperature control, the 2-hour/4-hour rule, allergens and cross-contamination, then sit a full timed run. Once you consistently score 80%+, you are ready to enrol with an accredited Victorian provider — see find a course.

VIC Food Safety FAQ

What is DoFoodSafely in Victoria?+
A free online food safety learning program run by the Victorian Department of Health. It is not a formal certificate but helps food handlers meet training requirements.
Is DoFoodSafely a recognised certificate?+
No. DoFoodSafely is a free knowledge program, not a nationally recognised certificate. For an FSS role you still need SITXFSA005/006 from an RTO.
Do I register my food business with the council in Victoria?+
Yes. Food businesses register or notify through their local council, and temporary or mobile vendors use the Streatrader portal.
Can I do FSS training online in Victoria?+
Yes. Many RTOs offer fully online FSS courses recognised in Victoria and nationally.
Who regulates food safety in Victoria?+
The Department of Health Victoria oversees food safety, with local councils conducting inspections.
