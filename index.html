The Temperature Danger Zone at a Glance

Temperature control is the single most-tested area of food safety. Keep potentially hazardous food out of the 5°C–60°C danger zone:

60°C+Hot-hold SAFE
DANGER ZONE5°C – 60°C
<5°CChilled SAFE
60°C+ · Hot-hold safe Keep displayed hot food at 60°C or above. Cook poultry and minced meat to 75°C; reheat to 70°C.
5°C – 60°C · Danger zone Bacteria multiply fastest here. 2-hour / 4-hour rule: under 2 h → chill or use; 2–4 h → use now; over 4 h → discard.
Below 5°C · Chilled safe Fridges at 5°C or below; freezers at −18°C or below. Bacterial growth slows significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Food Safety

What is food safety training in Australia? +
Food safety training covers hygienic food handling under SITXFSA005 (food handlers) and SITXFSA006 (Food Safety Supervisors). It aligns with the Australia New Zealand Food Standards Code and state food Acts.
Food Safety Practice Test Format and Topics +
Each practice test randomly selects 40 questions from our bank of 650 exam-style questions. Topics include temperature control, cross contamination, allergens, cleaning, HACCP, and FSS duties.
Is food safety certification valid in every Australian state? +
Yes. Nationally recognised units SITXFSA005 and SITXFSA006 are accepted across all states and territories. Each state has its own regulator (e.g. NSW Food Authority, Department of Health Victoria).
Is this the official food safety test? +
This practice test is for educational purposes only. We are not affiliated with FSANZ or any government agency. For official certification, complete training through an accredited RTO.
